How the round-1 script "identifies / scores" text
================================================================
Summary: no model, no AI — purely "counting signals with rules"


■ The core: read each text, add points when a signal is found
----------------------------------------------------------------
The script has a predefined list of words/patterns and checks whether the text contains these signals.
Each one found adds points:

  signal            | how it's checked                             | points
  ------------------|----------------------------------------------|----------
  praise word       | contains one of [ดี, เยี่ยม, สุดยอด, เลิศ, ฟิน...] |   +1
  sarcastic emoji   | contains 🙄 🙃 👏 ✨                          |   +2
  laughter 555      | contains 3+ consecutive "5"s                 |   +1
  elongation        | a letter repeated 3× in a row (e.g. มาก "กก") |   +1
  thanks            | contains ขอบคุณ/ขอบใจ                          |   +1
  praise + bad ctx  | a praise word "and" a negative word (รอ/นาน/พัง/แย่) together |   +3  <- strongest

  total = sum of all signals found in that text


■ A real counting example
----------------------------------------------------------------
text: "ร้านนี้ดีย์มากกก สั่งไป 40 นาทีของยังไม่มา 👏"
  - found praise word "ดี"                    -> +1
  - found emoji 👏                            -> +2
  - found letter repeat "กกก"                 -> +1
  - found praise "ดี" + negative "ไม่มา" together -> +3
  - total = 7  -> over the threshold (3) -> goes to the "high-suspicion" pile

text: "อร่อยจริง ราคาไม่แพง จะกลับมาอีก"
  - no words from the list -> 0 points -> normal pile


■ Why most items get 0-1
----------------------------------------------------------------
Because ordinary text has none of these signals (no over-the-top praise, no emoji, no 555),
so it gets 0, or a single signal gets 1 -> this is where the majority sit.
Only items with several signals at once shoot up to a high score and get pulled in as suspects.


■ "Identify" here = shortlist suspects, not decide sarcasm
----------------------------------------------------------------
The most important point: a high score "does not mean sarcasm."
It only means "enough signals that a human should take a look."
The real decision of sarcastic or not is still made by a human labeler.

Analogy: like a metal detector that beeps when it finds metal
but doesn't say whether it's gold or a coin — a human has to open it up and check.


■ Latest run (interpretation)
----------------------------------------------------------------
- Most texts get 0-1 (no signals) = normal
- The high-score pile is the pulled-in suspects
- Selected 400 items, mixing 240 high-suspicion / 160 normal as configured = works to spec

A caveat seen from the results: Wongnai reviews are long, so they naturally often contain both praise
and negatives in one story (recounting a long wait but good food), getting caught as "praise+bad"
even though they're normal reviews, not sarcasm -> this is normal for keyword filtering.
The real fix is to have a human label them 0, or adjust the script (drop long texts / focus on the shorter Wisesight).
