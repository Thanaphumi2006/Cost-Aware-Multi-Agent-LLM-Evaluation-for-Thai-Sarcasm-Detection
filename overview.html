Only pay the AI when it's actually needed

The program reads Thai restaurant reviews and decides: is this person being sarcastic? Someone writing “great service!” when they mean the opposite.

Asking OpenAI costs money every single time. So — can a free program handle the easy ones, and only pay for the hard ones?

Yes. Paying for only 4 sentences out of 10 gave us 95% of the quality of paying for all 10. Paying for the other 6 barely helped at all.

Try the free version — paste Thai text, runs in your browser, no sign-up and no API key.

The whole idea is an if-statement

A small free model reads the sentence and returns a number from 0 to 1 — how sarcastic it looks.

# free, runs on your own laptop
p = model.predict(text)

if p < 0.35:
    answer = "not sarcastic"   # sure → free
elif p > 0.65:
    answer = "sarcastic"       # sure → free
else:
    answer = ask_openai(text)   # not sure → pay

That's it. The rest of the project is figuring out how big that middle gap should be — small gap means cheap but more mistakes, big gap means you're paying full price again.

How good is each method?

Everything was tested on the same 127 sentences. Scores run 0 to 1, higher is better. Nothing gets close to 1 — sarcasm is genuinely hard.

The funny one: “555” is how Thai people type “hahaha”. Checking whether a sentence contains it — one line of code, no AI at all — beat every free AI model, including two built specially for Thai.

Worth knowing: only 127 sentences were tested, and just 30 of them were sarcastic. That's a small test, so small differences between methods might only be luck.
